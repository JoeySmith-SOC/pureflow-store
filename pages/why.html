The Science of Clean Water

Why Reverse Osmosis?

Tap water in the US is treated — but "treated" isn't the same as "pure." RO is the only home filtration technology that removes virtually all contaminants, including PFAS, microplastics, heavy metals, and bacteria simultaneously.

1

Pre-Filtration (Carbon Block)

Water first passes through activated carbon composite filters that remove chlorine, chloramines, sediment, and large organic molecules. This protects the RO membrane and improves taste at this stage alone.

2

Reverse Osmosis Membrane

Water is forced at pressure through a semi-permeable membrane with pores just 0.0001 microns — smaller than most viruses. PFAS, lead, arsenic, nitrates, and dissolved solids are rejected and flushed to drain. Pure water molecules pass through.

3

Post-Filtration (Polish)

A final carbon polish filter removes any remaining taste or odor compounds before the water reaches your faucet. Some systems also include UV sterilization at this stage, destroying 99.9% of bacteria and viruses.

4

Optional Remineralization

RO water is ultra-pure but can taste slightly flat. An optional remineralization filter adds calcium, magnesium, and potassium back — improving taste and adding health benefits back to every glass.
